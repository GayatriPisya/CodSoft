ABANINDRANATH TAGORE FAMOUS WORKS

"The Passing of Shah Jahan" is a famous painting by Abanindranath Tagore, a prominent Indian artist and the founder of the Bengal School of Art. Completed in 1902, this painting is notable for its evocative portrayal of the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, known for commissioning the Taj Mahal, during his final moments.

"Night at the Shalimar - The Emperor Shah Jahan" is a renowned painting by Abanindranath Tagore, a pivotal figure in the revival of Indian art and the founder of the Bengal School of Art. This painting captures a serene and reflective moment in the life of the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, known for his grand architectural achievements, most notably the Taj Mahal.

"Asoka's Queen" is a significant painting by Abanindranath Tagore, a pioneer of the Bengal School of Art. This piece portrays a poignant moment involving one of the queens of Emperor Ashoka, known for his transformation from a fierce conqueror to a devout Buddhist. Tagore's subtle use of color and delicate brushwork creates a serene and contemplative atmosphere, capturing the emotional depth and inner world of the queen. The painting reflects Tagore's mastery in blending traditional Indian styles with Persian and Japanese influences, offering a glimpse into the personal and historical narratives of India's past.

"Chashma Shahi" is one of the renowned works by Abanindranath Tagore, a pivotal figure in the Bengal School of Art. The painting captures the essence of Chashma Shahi, a beautiful Mughal garden located in Srinagar, Kashmir. This garden, also known as the Royal Spring, was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 AD.
